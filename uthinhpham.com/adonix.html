Propulsion

DESCRIPTION
Getting a rocket off the pad is a propulsion problem. Chemical engines like the Raptor and RS-25 still do the heavy lifting, but ion drives, Hall thrusters, and nuclear thermal concepts are quietly changing how we move through space. I follow the engineering trade-offs - thrust, specific impulse, mass fraction - that decide what missions are possible.
